A 15‑minute play routine that actually works (step by step)

Vet behaviorists and rehab specialists agree: short, high-intensity intervals with built-in rest are the sweet spot for canine weight loss. Here’s the exact framework you can follow today.

1. Start with a 2‑minute sniffari warm‑up

Let your dog sniff and walk slowly. This warms up joints and lowers cortisol. For apartment dwellers, a hallway or small patch of grass works perfectly.

2. The 30‑second sprint & rest cycle (repeat 8‑10 times)

This is where the magic happens. Use a toy that moves unpredictably — like a rolling ball or an automatic launcher set to short distance. Dog sprints for 10–15 seconds, then rests 20–30 seconds while the next ball loads. This natural interval pattern burns 2–3x more calories than a slow walk.

3. Cool‑down: 3 minutes of gentle walking & stretching

Encourage a slow pace, maybe some gentle massage. This prevents stiffness, especially in senior dogs.

💡 “Fifteen minutes of sprint‑rest intervals beats an hour of leash dragging. It’s the secret of canine fitness pros.”

What to avoid if your dog is overweight

❌ Sudden sharp turns on hard floors — use rugs or grass.
❌ Throwing balls high in the air (hard landings).
❌ Pushing through heavy panting — rest is part of the plan.
